Correlations Discovered Between Healthcare and, Income

From this data we can see that as poverty rates increase so does the lack of healthcare, although not in a uniform pattern.

The state with the lowest amount of healthcare is Texas yet there are 13 states with more poverty than them. The state with the highest amount of poverty is Mississippi yet there are two states with less healthcare than them.

With these results we can come to the conclusion that while poverty might not be the only factor leading to reduced healthcare it is one of if not the major contributing factors.
